# Dashboard Overview

## 1.Daily Active Wallets (Senders vs. Receivers)
Insight: There are a lot of "Senders" (blue) but very few "Receivers" (red). While the number of senders spikes and changes, the number of receivers stays very flat and low.

Why: This happens because thousands of people are sending money to the same few places (like a big exchange or a popular app). It’s like a city where 50,000 people drive to work, but they all go to the same 5 office buildings.

Takeaway: The network is "Hub-based." People aren't sending money to each other; they are all using a few giant "Super-Apps."



## 2.Whale Wallet Detection (Big Money)
Insight: Starting in 2025, the amount of money being moved (red spikes) grew massive, but the number of actual trades (blue line) didn't change much.

Why: The "Whales" (big investors) aren't trading more often—they are just moving much larger amounts in every trade.

Takeaway: This is now a "Big Money" network. Large institutions are moving billions of dollars, making the network more professional and less about small individual gambles.



## 3.Wallet Activity Distribution (Who is busiest?)
Insight: "Whale" wallets are doing almost all the work, with over 14 million transactions. Small and medium users are barely a fraction of that.

Why: Whales use automated bots that can trade hundreds of times a minute. A regular person might only make one or two trades a day.

Takeaway: This network is driven by automation. Most of the "action" you see is coming from computer programs, not humans clicking buttons.



## 4.Whale Withdrawal Destinations (The Safes)
Insight: Almost all the money leaving the network is flowing into just three or four specific addresses.

Why: These are likely "Cold Storage" (ultra-secure digital safes) or major exchanges. When Whales finish trading, they move their profits to these safe spots to protect them.

Takeaway: Wealth is very concentrated. A few "digital vaults" hold the majority of the money that moves off this blockchain.
